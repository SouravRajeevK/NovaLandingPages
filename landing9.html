How Nova's recommendation engine works

A transparent look at what happens when you complete the Nova assessment.

PHASE_01

Structured intake

~5 minutes

The assessment gathers structured data about your situation across four key dimensions: relationship and legal status, financial situation, property arrangements, and children/dependants. Each question is calibrated to capture the information required for an accurate recommendation without unnecessary burden.

Adaptive questioning End-to-end encryption No account required GDPR compliant
PHASE_02

Situational analysis

Automated

Nova's engine processes your responses against a comprehensive model of UK family law pathways, informed by 30+ years of Family Law Partners' case knowledge and continuously refined against outcomes data. The model identifies which of the six primary dispute resolution pathways — from direct agreement to litigation — are viable for your situation.

6 pathways evaluated Outcome-weighted model Real-time processing 30+ years of case data
PHASE_03

Recommendation output

Instant

Your recommendation is presented with full explanation — not just a pathway name, but the reasoning behind it, what it involves, what it typically costs, how long it takes, and what outcomes to expect. This transparency enables you to make a genuinely informed decision rather than simply following a black-box output.

Explainable outputs Cost estimates included Timeline projections Alternative options shown
PHASE_04

Expert connection (optional)

When you're ready

When you choose to engage an expert, Nova's matching algorithm identifies the most appropriate professional within Family Law Partners based on case type, complexity, location preference, and communication style. The professional receives a comprehensive briefing prior to your first meeting, eliminating the need to repeat your story and reducing the time — and cost — of initial consultations.

Expert pre-briefing Skills-based matching SRA regulated No obligation
